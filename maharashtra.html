AJANTA CAVES

Located in Maharashtra, the Ajanta Caves are a complex of ancient Buddhist rock-cut caves renowned for their exquisite frescoes and sculptures. Dating back to the 2nd century BCE, these caves depict the life of Buddha and are a UNESCO World Heritage site, offering a glimpse into India's ancient art and religious history.

ELLORA CAVES

Also in Maharashtra, the Ellora Caves are an impressive group of rock-cut caves representing Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain faiths. These caves, constructed between the 6th and 9th centuries, showcase intricate carvings and monolithic structures, including the remarkable Kailash Temple, making them a UNESCO World Heritage site.

ELEPHANTA CAVES

Situated on Elephanta Island near Mumbai, the Elephanta Caves are a collection of ancient rock-cut temples and sculptures dedicated to Lord Shiva. Dating from the 5th to 8th centuries, these caves feature remarkable bas-reliefs and a massive three-headed Shiva statue. They are a UNESCO World Heritage site and a testament to India's religious and artistic heritage.
